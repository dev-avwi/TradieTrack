Next is **not more features** — next is making JobRunner **release-ready** with proof.

Here’s the exact “ready to go” checklist in the right order (copy this into Replit as a sprint).

---

## 1) Lock the MVP scope for launch

**Freeze** anything that isn’t required for:

* create job → assign crew → track → complete → invoice → get paid → client can view

**Launch scope must be stable for 2–4 weeks** (no big feature changes).

---

## 2) Run the “Worst-Day Test” (must pass)

Build a test script and confirm these work end-to-end:

* ✅ No internet mid-job (offline queue works)
* ✅ Phone dies mid shift (clock out + audit recovery)
* ✅ GPS denied / inaccurate (fallback + labels)
* ✅ Subcontractor web-only flow works (OTP + uploads + timeline)
* ✅ Payment fails + webhook delayed (correct states, no double-charging)
* ✅ Duplicate account prevention (Apple/email merge prompts)
* ✅ Client portal OTP + “not found” handling

If any fail → not ready.

---

## 3) Implement the “Proof Pack” for disputes

One click export per job:

* timeline (arrived/started/finished)
* hours per worker
* materials + receipts
* before/after photos
* invoice PDF
  This is what owners need when clients argue.

---

## 4) Make onboarding “premium”

Before ads or App Store push:

* guided setup (business details → rates → logo → stripe → first job)
* demo data toggle (“try with sample job”)
* “view as client” preview

If onboarding feels icky, conversions die.

---

## 5) Mobile/Web parity audit

Create a parity checklist and tick every item:

* job create/edit
* assign crew/subbies
* timer + geofence
* photos/docs share-to-client
* quote/invoice generation
* client portal preview
* payments status

If web can do it but mobile can’t → fix it now.

---

## 6) Analytics + crash/error tracking

Before launch you need:

* signup funnel tracking
* “job created” / “invoice sent” / “paid” events
* crash reporting
* error logging for SMS, GPS, payments, syncing

Otherwise you’ll be blind.

---

## 7) Beta pilot with 5 real tradies

Not friends who “say it’s good”.
You want:

* 2 solo handyman
* 2 small teams (2–5)
* 1 bigger operator (8–15)

Give them:

* a simple onboarding sheet
* a WhatsApp/SMS feedback channel
* weekly 15 min check-in
  Track: what breaks + what confuses them.
