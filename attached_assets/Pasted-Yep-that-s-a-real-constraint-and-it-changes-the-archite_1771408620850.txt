Yep — that’s a real constraint, and it changes the architecture.

If you only have ONE JobRunner phone number total, you can still make it work, but you need to decide which model you’re aiming for:

Model A (works now, simpler): “Shared JobRunner Number” + smart routing

Model B (proper business-ready): “Dedicated number per business” (even if hidden/forwarded)

Here’s how to do both, and what I recommend.

⸻

Model A — One shared JobRunner number (works, but has limits)

How it works
	•	Every client texts the same number (your JobRunner number)
	•	Your system routes based on:
	1.	Client phone number → which business they belong to
	2.	Active job(s) under that business

What you MUST build to make this not messy
	1.	Incoming SMS must match a business

	•	Find client by fromPhone
	•	If that phone exists under multiple businesses (possible), ask:
“Reply 1 for Mike’s Plumbing, 2 for Dave’s Electrical”

	2.	Then match a job

	•	If exactly 1 active job: attach to that job thread
	•	If multiple: ask them to choose which job

	3.	Stop cross-tenant leaks

	•	Absolutely enforce tenant isolation so one client can’t ever land in the wrong business thread.

UX downsides (important)
	•	Clients see texts coming from “JobRunner” not “Mike’s Plumbing”
	•	Tradies may not like that branding
	•	Caller ID / trust is weaker
	•	Harder to scale to “serious businesses”

This model is OK for beta, family businesses, early traction.

⸻

Model B — Dedicated number per business (what “proper businesses” expect)

This is the standard in pro field-service systems: the business has its own texting number (even if it’s virtual).

How it works
	•	Each tradie business gets a unique number (Twilio number)
	•	Clients text that number
	•	Your system routes instantly: number → business → job thread
	•	You can optionally forward calls to their existing phone

Why it’s better
	•	Client sees “Mike’s Plumbing” comms feel direct
	•	Businesses trust it more
	•	Less routing ambiguity
	•	Much cleaner for compliance, brand, and scaling

Cost reality

Numbers are cheap compared to churn.
(You can include it only on Pro/Team tiers.)

⸻

What I recommend for you right now

Do this in phases:

Phase 1 (now, fast): Use your single JobRunner number for beta

✅ Build smart routing + job selection prompts
✅ Keep everything logged in Chat Hub
✅ Prove the system works

Phase 2 (when you onboard real businesses): Add “Business Numbers” as a paid upgrade

✅ Assign each business its own number
✅ Still keep your main JobRunner number for your own support

This is the best path: you don’t block progress now, but you don’t get stuck later.

How to make Model A feel decent (even with one number)

Message templates must clearly show the business identity

When your system sends a reply, it should start like:

“Mike’s Plumbing via JobRunner:”
then the message.

Also in portal:
	•	show business logo/name heavily
	•	use their brand colors

Give clients a portal link in the SMS

So they can click to see job/quote/invoice details (rich context).

Exact routing logic you should implement (for one shared number)

On inbound SMS:
	1.	fromPhone lookup:

	•	find all client records with that phone across all businesses

Cases:
	•	0 matches → “Which business are you messaging? Reply with business name or code”
	•	1 business match → proceed
	•	multiple business matches → ask them to pick

	2.	Determine job:

	•	if 1 active job in last 30 days → attach
	•	if multiple → ask:
“Reply 1 for Blocked drain (Smith St), 2 for Hot water (Lake St)”

	3.	Save message:

	•	conversationId = jobConversationId
	•	channel = sms
	•	businessId = resolvedBusinessId

	4.	Notify:

	•	assigned worker(s) + job manager + owner fallback
