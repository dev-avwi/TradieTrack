Yep — Visual Dispatch (and the “real business” feel) comes from one thing:

**Operational awareness.**
Everyone (owner/dispatch/crew/subbies) is looking at the *same job truth*, and the client sees the *safe version* of that truth.

So if you want TradieTrack/JobRunner to feel like Visual Dispatch, you need **Assets/Equipment + Crew Ops + Job Intelligence** layered onto your already-clean UI.

Here’s the exact feature set + how it connects across **Owner/Manager/Worker/Subcontractor/Client**.

---

## 1) The Visual Dispatch “Core” You’re Missing

### A) Assets / Equipment Tracking (big one)

**Goal:** know *what equipment is where*, who has it, and what job it’s on.

**Asset types**

* Equipment: generator, trencher, ladder, camera, pressure cleaner
* Vehicles: ute, van, trailer
* Tools (optional tier): high-value tracked tools only
* Hire items: rented gear with return date

**Asset fields**

* Asset ID / QR code
* Category + make/model
* Status: `Available / In Use / In Transit / Maintenance / Lost / Retired`
* Current holder: `user_id` (worker/subbie) OR `vehicle_id` OR `job_id`
* Current location (optional): last known GPS / depot / job address
* Next due: service date, compliance date
* Notes + photos

**Asset workflows**

* “Assign asset to job” (dispatch does it)
* “Check-out / Check-in” (worker scans QR)
* “Transfer asset” (worker → worker)
* “Maintenance flag” (worker taps “issue”)

✅ This is what makes larger businesses run smooth.

---

### B) Crew Ops on One Job (multi-person reality)

**Goal:** multiple workers/subbies can contribute without chaos.

**Per job you must support**

* Primary worker + support crew + subcontractors
* Per-person status: `En Route / Arrived / Working / Done`
* Per-person time tracking (auto rolls into job totals)
* Shared job notes + photos + documents
* Activity timeline (“who did what, when”)

**Crew view (internal)**

* Job card shows:

  * crew list + status dots
  * who’s en route
  * who uploaded photos
  * materials added by who
  * assets assigned

**Non-on-site workers**

* Can open job and see:

  * live status
  * asset list
  * photos/docs
  * timeline
  * “what’s next” checklist
* But they can’t edit if they’re not assigned (unless manager).

---

## 2) Make the Client’s Life Easy (without exposing everything)

Client sees a **clean portal view**, not internal mess.

### Public Tracking Link (/p/{job_token})

One map showing:

* destination pin
* **all assigned worker/subbie pins** (but privacy rules)
* per-person ETA (tap to select a worker)

Client also sees:

* job status
* arrival window
* before photos (if allowed)
* “Open verified portal” button

### Verified Client Portal (OTP)

Client sees:

* quotes / approvals
* invoices / pay
* job history
* documents
* photos
* **crew info in a controlled way**

  * Default: “Your crew is on the way”
  * Optional: show names (employees only)
  * Phones: usually dispatch only

✅ Client gets clarity, not complexity.

---

## 3) “Same Page” for Everyone: The Single Source of Truth

You need one job timeline that everything writes to:

**Event Timeline (append-only)**

* Worker pressed “On My Way”
* Subbie accepted
* Asset checked out (Generator #G-14)
* Material added (PVC 25mm)
* Before photos uploaded
* Manager approved timesheet
* Invoice generated
* Client paid

Internal roles see full timeline.
Client sees filtered timeline (no internal stuff like rates).

---

## 4) What to Add to Team Operations (so it scales)

### Roles

* Owner
* Manager/Dispatcher
* Worker
* Subcontractor
* Optional: Office/Admin

### Assignment rules

* Multiple assignments per job
* Must set `primary_assignment_id`
* Subcontractors must accept before acting
* Visibility flags per assignment:

  * show on client map? (default yes)
  * show name? (default: employee yes / subbie optional)
  * show phone? (default no)

### Dispatch views

* Board view (pipeline)
* Map view (live)
* Crew availability
* Asset availability

---

## 5) Materials “Visual Dispatch style”

Big companies need:

* Materials list with:

  * qty, unit cost, markup, supplier
  * receipt photo
  * “picked up” location (Bunnings etc.)
* “Material run” event:

  * logs travel + time + proof
* Auto invoice lines from materials

---

## 6) The Clean UI Trick (so it doesn’t become clutter)

Keep your UI simple by using **tabs** inside a job:

### Worker Job Screen tabs

* Overview (status + buttons)
* Crew (who’s on job + statuses)
* Assets (scan/check-out)
* Materials (quick add + receipt)
* Photos (before/after)
* Notes (checklist + timeline)

### Dispatch Job Screen tabs

* Overview
* Crew & assignments
* Assets & materials
* Timeline & approvals
* Quote/Invoice

Client portal is separate and simplified.
