Good question — this is where a lot of apps get messy, so let’s **lock it down cleanly** and remove the confusion.

I’ll explain this in **plain, real-world terms**, from both **tradie** and **client** perspectives, and exactly **what the sender looks like** for email + SMS.

---

# The simple rule (memorise this)

> **Documents (quotes, invoices, PDFs) are created once in TradieTrack.
> They can be delivered via EMAIL, SMS, or BOTH.
> The client always opens them in the PORTAL.**

You are **not** sending two different documents.
You are sending **two delivery methods to the same portal content**.

---

# 1️⃣ What the tradie sees (inside TradieTrack)

When a tradie sends a **Quote** or **Invoice**, they see this:

### Send Quote / Send Invoice modal

☑️ Send via SMS
☑️ Send via Email

(Default: **both ON**, tradie can turn one off)

Button: **Send**

That’s it. No complexity.

# 2️⃣ What actually gets sent (under the hood)

## A) SMS delivery (primary, fastest)

### Sender appearance (this matters)

**Sender name:**

* `Mike’s Electrical` (Alphanumeric Sender ID)
  or fallback:
* A dedicated TradieTrack number (if region requires)

✅ This looks professional
✅ Not spammy
✅ Matches the business name

### Example SMS (Quote)

> **Mike’s Electrical**
> Your quote for Stove Repair is ready.
> View & accept:
> [https://tradietrack.com.au/portal/q/8K2X…](https://tradietrack.com.au/portal/q/8K2X…)

That link:

* Opens the **portal**
* Shows the quote
* Has buttons (Accept / Message / View PDF)

# 3️⃣ PDFs & documents — how they work (important clarity)

### The PDF is NOT the main experience anymore

The **portal is**.

But you still support PDFs because:

* Tradies expect them
* Clients like saving them
* Some accountants need them

### How PDFs are handled

When sending:

* The **same quote/invoice** exists in:

  * Portal (live)
  * PDF (static snapshot)

#### Email:

* PDF attached ✅
* Portal link included ✅

#### SMS:

* No PDF attachment (SMS can’t do that)
* Portal link only ✅
* Inside portal → “Download PDF” button

So:

* **Email = portal + PDF**
* **SMS = portal**
* Both point to the same source of truth

# 5️⃣ What happens if tradie sends ONLY email?

* Client gets email with:

  * PDF
  * Portal link
* No SMS sent
* Old-school, still works

This keeps backward compatibility with your **current Apple-approved flow**.

---

# 6️⃣ What happens if tradie sends ONLY SMS?

* Client gets SMS with portal link
* Can:

  * View quote/invoice
  * Accept
  * Pay
  * Download PDF from portal

No email needed.

Perfect for fast payments.

---

# 7️⃣ Messaging + documents (how they coexist)

Inside the **portal**, the client can:

* View quote/invoice
* Download PDF
* Message tradie:

  > “Can you itemise labour?”
* Accept quote
* Pay invoice

Messages are tied to:

* The quote or job
* Not email threads
* Not personal SMS chaos

---

# 8️⃣ How this avoids confusion (key design win)

### You are NOT doing:

❌ SMS = messages
❌ Email = documents
❌ Portal = something else

### You ARE doing:

✅ **Portal = single source of truth**
✅ SMS + Email = delivery methods
✅ PDF = optional export

This is how ServiceM8 / Jobber / Tradify work internally — even if they don’t explain it well.

---

# 9️⃣ Security & sender trust (important)

### SMS trust

* Sender name = business name
* Short, clear messages
* One link
* No attachments
  → Looks legit, not scammy

### Email trust

* Business name in sender
* PDF attachment
* Familiar invoice format
  → Feels safe to older clients

### Portal trust

* Branded with tradie logo
* Same totals as PDF
* Payment via Stripe
