It won’t interfere — it actually makes the whole system cleaner and stronger, as long as you keep one rule:

SMS is for job/invoice updates and urgent stuff.
The website is for getting new leads and credibility.

They do different jobs, so they don’t clash.

Why it makes things better

1) Website → cleaner lead capture

Instead of tradies getting random calls/DMs, the website form drops enquiries straight into TradieTrack as Leads (tagged “Website”).
That means you can then run your normal pipeline:
Lead → Quote → Job → Invoice → Pay.

2) SMS workflow stays exactly the same (and becomes more valuable)

Your SMS flow is triggered by job actions inside TradieTrack:
	•	Quote sent
	•	On My Way
	•	Invoice sent
	•	(later reminders, overdue, etc.)

The website doesn’t touch those triggers. It just creates leads that feed into them.

So the website basically increases the number of jobs entering your SMS automation flow.

3) Fewer “where did that enquiry come from?” headaches

Because the lead is created inside TradieTrack, you can show:
	•	Source = Website
	•	Timestamp
	•	Client details
	•	Notes

This makes follow-up + conversion easier.

4) Portal links remain magic-link only (no conflict)

Clients still access the portal via links TradieTrack sends during:
	•	quote
	•	job
	•	invoice

Your website can have a “Client Portal” button, but it doesn’t generate access. So no security issue and no confusion if you label it properly (e.g., “Portal link is sent by SMS after booking”).

⸻

The only ways it could interfere (and how to avoid it)

Risk A: Website form triggers SMS to the client too early

If your website lead form automatically texts the client “here’s your portal link”… that’s where confusion starts.

✅ Fix:
Don’t send portal links at lead stage.
Only send portal links when:
	•	quote is sent, or
	•	job is created, or
	•	invoice is sent.

(Exactly like your spec.)

Risk B: Tradie starts messaging outside TradieTrack and expects it to be tracked

If a tradie replies from their personal SMS to a website enquiry, it won’t be logged.

✅ Fix:
Keep it simple in the app:
	•	Lead is in TradieTrack
	•	Tradie can call/text from their phone if they want
	•	Once job starts, TradieTrack SMS + portal become the official thread

That’s a natural split and it works in the real world.

⸻

The clean combined workflow (best version)
	1.	Customer visits tradie website → submits “Get a Quote”
	2.	Lead appears in TradieTrack (source: Website)
	3.	Tradie calls/texts (manual) to confirm details (optional)
	4.	Tradie sends quote through TradieTrack → SMS goes out with quote link
	5.	Quote accepted → job scheduled → (optional reminders later)
	6.	On My Way → SMS with link
	7.	Invoice sent → SMS with pay link
	8.	Everything saved in job history

So: website feeds the machine, SMS runs the job.
