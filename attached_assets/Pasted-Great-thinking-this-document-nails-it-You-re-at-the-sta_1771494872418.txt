Great thinking - this document nails it. You're at the stage where polish beats power. Let me lay out exactly what I'd do for each of these modules, keeping it clean and non-messy.

Here's my breakdown:

1. Compliance & Licensing Module (under Business Tools / Settings area)

This would be a new "Compliance" tab accessible only to owners. It tracks:

Licence cards - trade licence number, expiry date, type (electrical, plumbing, building, etc.)
Insurance policies - public liability, professional indemnity, workers comp - with expiry dates
White cards / safety tickets - per worker, with expiry tracking
Vehicle rego - linked to equipment if you want, or standalone
Alerts - automatic dashboard warnings when anything expires within 30 days
PDF export - one-click "Compliance Pack" PDF showing all current licences/insurance for sending to head contractors
I'd add a compliance_documents table (type, name, number, expiryDate, status, attachmentUrl, assignedToWorkerId). The dashboard would show an amber/red alert strip when something's expiring. Workers never see this tab at all.

2. Equipment & Asset Tracker Enhancement

You already have the equipment registry and basic utilisation. I'd add:

Service intervals - set km or hour-based service schedules per equipment item (e.g., "service every 500 hours")
Repair log - timestamped entries with cost, description, who did the repair
Running cost tracking - total spend per asset (purchase + repairs + fuel/consumables)
Profit per asset - revenue from jobs using that equipment minus its running costs
Service due alerts - integrated into the dashboard Ops Health Banner
This builds on your existing equipment, equipment_maintenance, and job_equipment tables. I'd add a service_intervals concept to the maintenance schema and calculate profit from job revenue vs equipment costs.

3. Pricing Intelligence (AI Quote Enhancement)

This is the smartest one because it's zero new UI - just an enhancement inside the existing Quote Editor. When you're creating a quote:

AI analyzes your past completed jobs with similar descriptions/trade categories
Shows a subtle suggestion card: "Based on 8 similar jobs, average was $14,200 with 28% margin"
Shows price range (low/high) from your own data
Optional: suggests line items from your most common items for that job type
I'd add a new endpoint /api/ai/pricing-intelligence that queries your completed jobs + invoices by trade category and job type keywords, calculates averages, and returns suggestions. The UI would be a small collapsible panel in the quote editor - not a new page.

4. Digital Job Diary (Construction Mode)

This only shows for trades tagged as Builder, Construction, Commercial, or Civil. It adds a "Daily Log" tab inside the existing Job Detail view:

Date-stamped entries: weather, workers on site, hours, work completed, materials delivered
Photo attachments per entry
Safety observations / incidents
Visitor log
Superintendent/client sign-off (digital signature)
I'd create a job_diary_entries table and render the tab conditionally based on the business's trade category. Plumbers, electricians, cleaners - they never see it. It's context-based UI, exactly as the document describes.

5. Dashboard & Core Hardening (the most important one)

Dashboard cleanup - reduce visual noise, make KPIs clearer, ensure every widget links somewhere useful
Notification reliability - audit all notification paths (push, SMS, email) and make sure nothing gets lost
Chat routing - ensure two-way SMS works properly once you have a Twilio number, test the routing state machine
Dispatch polish - schedule conflict detection, drag-drop reliability
