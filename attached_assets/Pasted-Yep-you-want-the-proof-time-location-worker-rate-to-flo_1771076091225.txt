Yep — you want the proof (time + location + worker/rate) to flow all the way through into quotes → invoices → client portal, automatically.

Here’s the clean way to spec it to Replit so it’s reliable and looks professional.

⸻

1) What must appear automatically on an Invoice

Invoice header (standard)
	•	Business name + logo
	•	ABN (optional)
	•	Owner name + phone/email
	•	Client name + address
	•	Job name + job address
	•	Invoice number + date + due date
	•	Payment details

Labour section (auto from time tracking)

For each worker (or aggregated), show:
	•	Worker name (optional toggle for client)
	•	Role (employee/subcontractor) (internal)
	•	Hourly rate (auto)
	•	Hours worked (auto)
	•	Labour subtotal (auto)

Example layout
	•	Labour — Ayden Vogler — $85/hr — 6.25 hrs — $531.25
	•	Labour — Worker 2 — $70/hr — 3.00 hrs — $210.00

✅ Requirement: the invoice must pull rate from the worker profile or job assignment rate override.

⸻

2) How worker rate should work (so it’s bulletproof)

You need 3 layers, in order of priority:

Rate priority order
	1.	Job assignment rate override (highest priority)
	2.	Worker default rate (from worker profile)
	3.	Company default rate (fallback)

So if someone’s a subcontractor or a specialist, you can override just for that job.

⸻

3) Time tracking → Timesheet → Invoice pipeline

Data model
	•	TimeEntry (raw logs): clock in/out, edits, GPS loss flags
	•	TimesheetSummary (computed): hours per worker per job per day
	•	InvoiceLineItems (final): labour lines + materials lines + discounts + GST

✅ Rule: never calculate invoice totals directly from raw logs every time.
Compute a stable “timesheet summary”, then invoice uses that.

⸻

4) Proof on the Invoice (GPS/Geofence)

You have two options depending on how “legal proof” you want it.

Option A (Clean & Client-Friendly)

Show a simple summary:
	•	“Work period: 7:58am – 3:42pm”
	•	“Total billable hours: 6.25”
	•	“Location: [Job Address]”
	•	“Tracking summary: Verified”

✅ This keeps it readable.

Option B (Detailed Proof Attachment)

Invoice includes a link or attachment:
	•	“View time & location log” (portal link)
	•	Includes:
	•	arrival/departure
	•	GPS accuracy
	•	GPS signal loss notes
	•	manual edit logs

✅ This is what Nathan wanted for disputes.

Best practice:
Keep Option A on invoice PDF, and Option B as a link/attachment.

⸻

5) Quotes vs Invoices (important)

Quotes should NOT show time tracking

Quotes are estimates. Instead show:
	•	Estimated labour hours (manual estimate by owner/manager)
	•	Rate
	•	Materials allowance
	•	Terms

But once job starts, the quote can convert to an invoice where actual tracked time replaces estimates.

⸻

6) The “auto rate” + “auto time” rules you want

Invoice generation rules

When generating invoice:
	1.	Gather all time entries for job between:
	•	job start date/time → job completion
	2.	Group by worker
	3.	Apply rate priority rules (job override → worker default → company default)
	4.	Round time consistently (you choose):
	•	nearest 5 minutes OR
	•	nearest 0.1 hour OR
	•	exact to minute (recommended for fairness)
	5.	Produce labour line items per worker
	6.	Add materials line items (from materials list)
	7.	Add optional travel charge line item (if enabled)
	8.	Generate PDF + portal link

On invoice PDF:
	•	Labour summary
	•	Worker rate + hours
	•	“Work performed at job address”
	•	Optional “Tracking report link”

In portal link:
	•	Timeline
	•	GPS travel map (On My Way only)
	•	Arrival time / departure time
	•	Before/after photos
	•	Worker + owner contacts
	•	Download invoice, quote, certificates

⸻

9) One key decision you need to pick

How do you want time to round?
	•	Exact minutes (most fair, most accurate)
	•	Nearest 5 minutes (industry common)
	•	Nearest 15 minutes (some tradies do this)

If you don’t choose, do nearest 5 minutes by default.
