## The baseline “must-match” feature set (to be taken seriously)

If JobRunner wants to compete “on par or above”, these are table-stakes across the leaders:

* **Quote → job → invoice** pipeline (fast, on mobile)
* **Scheduling/dispatch** (calendar, team allocation)
* **Job notes, photos, checklists, signatures**
* **Payments** (or at least clean Stripe/Xero workflows)
* **Accounting integrations** (Xero is huge in AU/NZ)
* **Customer comms** (SMS/email)
* **Reporting** (even if basic)
* **Multi-user/team support**
* Optional but high-value: **inventory/parts**, **assets/equipment registers**, **customer portal**, **automations**

That set is basically what these products position around. ([servicem8.com][1])

# 1) ServiceM8

### What it’s strongest at

**Field-first mobile workflow.** It’s designed to let tradies quote/invoice onsite and capture job proof (photos, signatures, notes). ([servicem8.com][1])

### Big features you’re competing with

* Job & staff management, dispatch/scheduling
* Onsite quoting/invoicing + PDF generation
* Signatures, job documentation, photos/videos
* “Track my arrival” style client comms
* It’s also pushing “AI” in invoicing (Auto-Invoice) ([servicem8.com][1])

### Common problems (from reviews)

* Reports of **glitches / reliability issues** in some features and support limitations in resolving issues quickly ([Software Advice][2])
* On G2, users mention **clunky/time-consuming invoicing navigation** and wanting a better customer portal / customization ([G2][3])

**Takeaway for JobRunner:** if you want to beat ServiceM8, you win on **reliability + speed of invoicing + better portal + clearer support**—while staying mobile-first.

# 2) Tradify

### What it’s strongest at

**“Simple, works, quote-to-invoice” for small teams** (often marketed for 1–20 people). ([App Store][4])

### Big features you’re competing with

* Quoting + invoicing, branded docs, automated payment chasing (“smart invoices”)
* Job management + scheduling
* Timesheets/appointments workflows for time & materials invoicing ([tradifyhq.com][5])

### Common problems (from reviews)

* **Cost/value concerns**, plus **limited reporting/customisation** and automation gaps mentioned in reviews ([Software Advice][6])
* Scheduling getting visually busy as team size grows (harder to see workload at a glance) ([Software Advice][6])
* Some users complain about **device/platform quirks** (e.g., Chromebook experience) ([Software Advice][6])
* Trustpilot review snippets include complaints around **Stripe fees / payment costs** ([Trustpilot][7])

**Takeaway for JobRunner:** win on **better reporting dashboards + better scheduling clarity + deeper customisation** without making it complex.

# 3) simPRO

### What it’s strongest at

**Big, enterprise-style trade ops**: jobs + projects, deeper inventory/purchasing, stronger integrations. ([Simpro][8])

### Big features you’re competing with

* Inventory tracking + purchase orders and pushing supplier invoices to accounting ([Simpro][9])
* Invoicing + accounting integrations workflows ([Simpro][8])
* Add-ons and integrations ecosystem ([Simpro][10])

### Common problems (from reviews)

* Reviews describe it as **time-consuming / needing heavy data entry**, with complaints about onboarding and support quality ([Software Advice][11])
* Reports of **inflexibility/customisation pain** and confusing invoice/tax presentation, plus inconsistent reports ([Capterra][12])

**Takeaway for JobRunner:** don’t try to “out-enterprise” simPRO initially. Beat it with **simplicity, faster onboarding, fewer clicks, cleaner UX**, while still offering core depth.

# 4) AroFlo

### What it’s strongest at

**Feature breadth**: scheduling, inventory, assets, projects, quoting, subcontractors, reporting, integrations. ([aroflo.com][13])

### Big features you’re competing with

* Job management and workflow automation (including email-to-job import) ([aroflo.com][14])
* Broader “platform” toolset (assets/property data/inventory/projects) ([aroflo.com][13])

### Common problems (from reviews)

* “**Overcomplicated / hard to learn**” and “too many fields we don’t use; can’t hide/turn off” ([Software Advice][15])
* Reviews also mention **bugs / value concerns** on some platforms ([Capterra][16])
* G2 mentions template/reporting not being straightforward and workflow quirks (e.g., purchasing steps) ([G2][17])

**Takeaway for JobRunner:** your edge is **customisability without clutter** (“hide fields/modules”, role-based UI, clean defaults).

## The most consistent “pain themes” across all 4 (with evidence)

If you want the *real* “where they suck”, the recurring themes from reviews are:

1. **Complexity / too many clicks / steep learning curve** (simPRO, AroFlo) ([Software Advice][11])
2. **Reporting & dashboards not good enough** (Tradify, simPRO, AroFlo users mention reporting difficulty/limitations) ([Software Advice][6])
3. **Support / onboarding pain** (simPRO onboarding complaints; ServiceM8 support complaints) ([Software Advice][11])
4. **Bugs / reliability** (ServiceM8, AroFlo reviews mention issues) ([Software Advice][2])
5. **Scheduling visibility at scale** (Tradify scheduler “busy” when team grows) ([Software Advice][6])

Also: your earlier “they aren’t AI-smart yet” needs updating—**ServiceM8 is actively marketing AI-based Auto-Invoice**. ([servicem8.com][18])
