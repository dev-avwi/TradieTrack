# 🔒 1. Design for the WORST DAY, not the BEST DAY

Tradies don’t judge software when things go smoothly.
They judge it when:

• Phone battery dies
• No signal on site
• Client is angry
• Worker forgets to clock out
• Subcontractor ghosts
• Invoice is disputed
• GPS is wrong
• Payment fails
• Owner is stressed

Your app must **not collapse** in those moments.

So you build for them intentionally.

---

# 🧠 2. “Nothing Breaks” Principles (Non-Negotiable)

### Principle 1: **No Data Loss**

* Offline queue for:

  * time
  * photos
  * materials
  * notes
* Clear sync indicators
* Retry until success

### Principle 2: **Nothing Is Permanent Without Approval**

* Time edits create audit entries
* Subcontractor hours need approval
* Invoice drafts before sending
* Payment confirmation before marking paid

### Principle 3: **Every Action Is Logged**

* Append-only event log
* Visible timeline for owner
* Exportable if dispute happens

This protects the business legally.

---

# 🚧 3. Handle the Toughest Real-World Tradie Scenarios

Below are **real nightmare scenarios** and how JobRunner must handle them.

---

## 🔥 Scenario 1: Worker forgets to clock out

**Solution**

* Auto-prompt when leaving geofence
* End-of-day reminder
* Manager can edit with reason
* Edit logged visibly

Owner sees:

> “Edited by manager – forgot to clock out”

---

## 🔥 Scenario 2: No signal on site

**Solution**

* Full offline mode
* Queue actions
* Show “Pending sync”
* Auto-sync later

User never wonders:

> “Did it save?”

---

## 🔥 Scenario 3: Client disputes invoice time

**Solution**

* Invoice can show:

  * time breakdown
  * arrival/finish timestamps
* Owner can attach:

  * job timeline
  * photos
* Client portal shows summary only

Trust restored.

---

## 🔥 Scenario 4: Subcontractor doesn’t install app

**Solution**

* Web-only subcontractor mode
* OTP verification
* Limited permissions
* Manual hours + approval

No excuses.

---

## 🔥 Scenario 5: Two workers on same job

**Solution**

* One tracking link
* Multi-pin map
* Individual time tracking
* Combined invoice summary

No confusion.

---

## 🔥 Scenario 6: Payment fails

**Solution**

* Clear status:

  * Failed
  * Retrying
  * Needs action
* Auto-notify owner
* Retry option
* Manual override (logged)

---

## 🔥 Scenario 7: Owner needs proof

**Solution**

* Export:

  * job timeline
  * photos
  * time logs
  * material receipts
* One PDF click

This is huge for disputes.

# ✨ 4. Polish Comes From Micro-Details

This is where apps feel *premium*.

### Micro-Polish Checklist

* Loading skeletons (never blank screens)
* Clear empty states (“No jobs yet — create your first”)
* Undo buttons for destructive actions
* Confirmation for irreversible actions
* Clear error messages (not “Something went wrong”)
* Consistent language everywhere

---

# 📱 5. Mobile Experience Must Be Calm Under Stress

Tradies use phones with:

* dirty hands
* gloves
* sun glare
* shaky signal

So:

* Big tap targets
* Minimal typing
* One-tap actions
* Offline first
* Haptic feedback
* Dark mode

Mobile must feel **calm**, not busy.

---

# 🧪 6. Quality Assurance: How You KNOW It’s Solid

You don’t guess. You prove.

### Required QA Strategy

#### A) “Worst Day Test”

Simulate:

* No internet
* Phone dies mid-job
* GPS disabled
* Invoice edited after sent
* Payment webhook delayed

If app survives → it’s ready.

---

#### B) Role-Based Test

Run same job as:

* Owner
* Manager
* Worker
* Subcontractor
* Client

Confirm:

* Everyone sees correct data
* No leakage
* No missing steps

---

#### C) Cross-Platform Test

* Start job on mobile
* Finish on web
* Invoice on mobile
* Payment on web
* Check timeline consistency
