Great questions! Let me explain each of these features:

üì± Tap to Pay on iPhone
What it is: Apple's NFC payment feature that lets you accept contactless card payments directly on your iPhone - no card reader hardware needed. Customer taps their card/phone on your iPhone screen and payment is done.

ServiceM8 has it: In AU, UK, US, Canada - tradies can collect payments on-site instantly.

What we have: QR codes (customer scans and pays via Stripe link) and payment links (SMS/email). Works but requires an extra step from the customer.

To add: Would need Stripe Terminal SDK integration with Apple's Tap to Pay APIs.

üìç "On My Way" Auto-SMS
What it is: When a technician checks out of one job or starts driving to the next, the system automatically sends an SMS to the upcoming customer: "Hi [Name], your technician Mike is on the way and should arrive in approximately 15 minutes."

Why it's valuable: Reduces "where are you?" calls, improves customer experience, feels professional.

What we have: Manual SMS sending - tradie has to type and send themselves.

To add: Trigger SMS when job status changes to "en route" or when GPS detects movement toward next job location.

üìã Job Queue System
What it is: A holding area for jobs that need attention but aren't ready to schedule yet. Like:

"Waiting for parts"
"Awaiting customer approval"
"Needs follow-up quote"
"Ready to invoice"
Jobs sit in queues until the blocker is resolved, then get scheduled.

What we have: Status-based filtering (you can filter by status) but no dedicated queue view with custom queue types.

üìÑ Supplier Invoice Import
What it is: You photograph or upload a supplier invoice (from Bunnings, electrical wholesaler, etc.), and the system:

OCR scans the invoice
Extracts parts/materials and costs
Auto-matches them to the job they belong to
Updates job costing with actual material costs
Why it's valuable: Accurate job profitability tracking without manual data entry.

üé§ Voice-Activated AI Assistant
What it is: Instead of typing, you speak: "Hey ServiceM8, schedule the Smith job for tomorrow at 2pm and send them a confirmation" - and it does it.

What we have: Text-based AI chat for suggestions and analysis.

To add: Would need speech-to-text integration + natural language command processing.
